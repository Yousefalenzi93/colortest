### 2. ✅ Fast Blue B Salt Test (NEW)
**Test ID:** `fast-blue-b-salt-test`  
**Target Substances:** Cannabis, THC  
**Color Result:** Blue to purple

**Preparation Steps:**
```
1. Place a small amount of the suspected material in a test tube.
2. Add a small amount of reagent 5A (Carefully mix 2.5 g of fast blue B salt with 100 g of anhydrous sodium sulfate).
3. Add 25 drops of reagent 5B (Chloroform) and shake the test tube for one minute.
4. Add 25 drops of reagent 5C (Dissolve 0.4 g of sodium hydroxide in 100 ml of water (= 0.1N sodium hydroxide solution)) and, again, shake for two minutes.
```

### 3. ✅ Duquenois-Levine Test (NEW)
**Test ID:** `duquenois-levine-test`  
**Target Substances:** Cannabis, THC  
**Color Result:** Purple to violet

**Preparation Steps:**
```
1. Place a small amount of the suspected material in a test tube.
2. Add 2 ml (ca. 50 drops) of reagent 6A (Dissolve 2 g of vanillin in 100 ml of 95% ethanol, then add 2.5 ml of acetaldehyde) and shake the test tube for one minute.
3. Add 2 ml of reagent 6B (Concentrated hydrochloric acid), shake again for a few seconds and allow the mixture to stand for a few minutes.
4. If a colour develops within 2-3 minutes, add 2 ml of reagent 6C (Chloroform) and shake the mixture gently.
```

### 4. ✅ Modified Cobalt Thiocyanate (Scott Test) (NEW)
**Test ID:** `modified-cobalt-thiocyanate-test`  
**Target Substances:** Cocaine  
**Color Result:** Blue → Pink → Blue

**Preparation Steps:**
```
1. Place a small amount of the suspected material in a test tube.
2. Add five drops of reagent 8A (Dissolve 1 g of cobalt(II) thiocyanate in 50 ml of 10% (vol/vol) acetic acid, then add 50 ml of glycerine) and shake the test tube for ten seconds. If cocaine is present a blue colour develops immediately. If no blue colour appears, add an additional amount of suspected material equal to the amount first used. If a blue colour still does not develop, the suspected material does not contain cocaine.
3. If the solution turned blue in step 2, add one drop of reagent 8B (Concentrated hydrochloric acid) and shake the mixture for a few seconds. The blue colour should now turn pink if cocaine is present. If the colour change is incomplete, add one additional drop of reagent 8B.
4. If the solution turns completely to pink in step 3, add five drops of reagent 8C (Chloroform) and shake again to mix the liquids. The blue colour should reappear in the lower (chloroform) layer indicating the presence of cocaine.
```

### 5. ✅ Wagner Test (NEW)
**Test ID:** `wagner-test`  
**Target Substances:** Alkaloids  
**Color Result:** Brown precipitate

**Preparation Steps:**
```
1. Place a small amount of the suspected material in a test tube.
2. Add five drops of water and shake the test tube for a few seconds.
3. Add two drops of reagent 10 (Mix 1.27 g of iodine and 2 g of potassium iodide, then dissolve the mixture in 100 ml of water).
```

### 6. ✅ Simon Test (NEW)
**Test ID:** `simon-test`  
**Target Substances:** MDMA, MDA, Amphetamines  
**Color Result:** Blue

**Preparation Steps:**
```
1. Place a small amount of the suspected material on a spot plate.
2. Add one drop of reagent 12A (Dissolve 0.9 g of sodium nitroprusside in 90 ml of water, then add 10 ml of acetaldehyde).
3. Add two drops of reagent 12B (Dissolve 2 g of sodium carbonate in 100 ml of water).
```

### 7. ✅ Ehrlich Test (NEW)
**Test ID:** `ehrlich-test`  
**Target Substances:** LSD, Psilocybin, DMT  
**Color Result:** Purple to violet

**Preparation Steps:**
```
1. Place a small amount of the suspected material on a spot plate.
2. Add two drops of reagent 20 (Dissolve 1 g of 4-dimethylamine benzaldehyde in 10 ml of methanol, then carefully add 10 ml of concentrated ortho-phosphoric acid).
```

## 🔬 Reagent Reference Guide

### Reagent 4: Concentrated Nitric Acid
- **Use:** Nitric Acid Test
- **Safety:** Highly corrosive, use with extreme caution

### Reagent 5A: Fast Blue B Salt Mixture
- **Composition:** 2.5 g fast blue B salt + 100 g anhydrous sodium sulfate
- **Use:** Fast Blue B Salt Test

### Reagent 5B: Chloroform
- **Use:** Fast Blue B Salt Test, Duquenois-Levine Test
- **Safety:** Toxic, use in well-ventilated area

### Reagent 5C: 0.1N Sodium Hydroxide
- **Composition:** 0.4 g NaOH in 100 ml water
- **Use:** Fast Blue B Salt Test

### Reagent 6A: Vanillin-Acetaldehyde Solution
- **Composition:** 2 g vanillin in 100 ml 95% ethanol + 2.5 ml acetaldehyde
- **Use:** Duquenois-Levine Test

### Reagent 6B: Concentrated Hydrochloric Acid
- **Use:** Duquenois-Levine Test
- **Safety:** Highly corrosive

### Reagent 6C: Chloroform
- **Use:** Duquenois-Levine Test
- **Safety:** Toxic, use in well-ventilated area

### Reagent 8A: Cobalt Thiocyanate Solution
- **Composition:** 1 g cobalt(II) thiocyanate in 50 ml 10% acetic acid + 50 ml glycerine
- **Use:** Scott Test

### Reagent 8B: Concentrated Hydrochloric Acid
- **Use:** Scott Test
- **Safety:** Highly corrosive

### Reagent 8C: Chloroform
- **Use:** Scott Test
- **Safety:** Toxic, use in well-ventilated area

### Reagent 10: Iodine-Potassium Iodide Solution
- **Composition:** 1.27 g iodine + 2 g potassium iodide in 100 ml water
- **Use:** Wagner Test

### Reagent 12A: Sodium Nitroprusside-Acetaldehyde Solution
- **Composition:** 0.9 g sodium nitroprusside in 90 ml water + 10 ml acetaldehyde
- **Use:** Simon Test

### Reagent 12B: Sodium Carbonate Solution
- **Composition:** 2 g sodium carbonate in 100 ml water
- **Use:** Simon Test

### Reagent 20: Ehrlich Reagent
- **Composition:** 1 g 4-dimethylamine benzaldehyde in 10 ml methanol + 10 ml concentrated ortho-phosphoric acid
- **Use:** Ehrlich Test
- **Safety:** Corrosive, handle with care
